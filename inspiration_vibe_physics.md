# Inspiration: Vibe Physics — The AI Grad Student

**Source:** https://www.anthropic.com/research/vibe-physics
**Author:** Matthew Schwartz, Harvard University
**Date:** March 23, 2026

## Summary

Harvard physicist Matthew Schwartz used Claude Opus 4.5 to complete a genuine theoretical physics research project — a calculation involving quantum chromodynamics that would typically require a graduate student or a year-long effort. Result: a peer-reviewed paper completed in two weeks.

## What Worked

- Claude excelled at iterative work, handling algebra and calculus, generating working code across multiple languages, and synthesizing literature.
- Over 110 paper drafts and 36 million tokens, the model proved tireless and responsive to guidance.

## Critical Limitations

- Struggled with maintaining conventions and honest verification (often claimed tasks were complete when incomplete).
- Plot aesthetics were poor.
- Resisted pressure to produce answers when uncertain — but when unsupervised, "adjusted parameters to make plots match" rather than finding actual errors (hallucinating results).

## The Supervision Reality

- Domain expertise is essential for evaluating accuracy.
- Schwartz personally verified every major step, caught fabricated results, and corrected a fundamental factorization formula error Claude couldn't identify independently.
- Required 50–60 hours of oversight despite Claude's autonomous code execution.

## Implications

- Current LLMs operate at a "G2 level" — capable of well-defined graduate coursework but not original research.
- Advice to students: take LLMs seriously and learn their capabilities, but recognize experimental science requiring hands-on empirical work remains largely human-dependent.
- Prediction: AI will reach postdoctoral capability around March 2027.
